# Pebble. — Brand Identity

## The Name

Pebble.

Not "Pebble" — Pebble with a period. The dot is part of the name. The dot IS the brand.

## Why Pebble.

### The metaphor
A pebble is small, smooth, and calm. You hold it in your hand when you're anxious. You skip it across water. It's grounding. It's the opposite of a mountain — it's the smallest, most manageable unit of something. That's exactly what the app does: takes mountains and turns them into pebbles.

### The dot
The period means:
- **Stop.** Pause. Breathe. You don't have to keep going.
- **A full stop.** This is complete. One thing done. Period.
- **The pebble itself.** Visually, a dot IS a pebble. The smallest possible shape. The logo is the punctuation.
- **Calm punctuation.** Not an exclamation mark (that's pressure). Not a question mark (that's uncertainty). A period. Definitive. Quiet. Done.
- **It's the mascot.** The dot appears everywhere — in greetings ("Good morning, Diego."), in nudges ("You did something. That counts."), in the app name itself. It's consistent, recognizable, and subtle.

### How it shows up
- Logo: "Pebble." — clean typography, the dot is slightly emphasized (maybe larger, maybe a different color, maybe the accent teal)
- Marketing: "Pebble. A calm place to start."
- Domain: Pebble.ai (or usepebble.ai, getpebble.app, etc.)
- In the app: Every AI message ends with a period. Every greeting uses it. "Good afternoon, Diego." "You did something. That counts." "One thing at a time." The dot is the voice.
- The dot could be the app icon — literally just a circle in the brand color on a warm background

### Why it's better than NeuroFocus
- NeuroFocus sounds clinical, like a medical device or brain scanning company
- Pebble sounds approachable, warm, human
- NeuroFocus tells you what it does (focus for neuro people). Pebble tells you how it feels (small, calm, manageable)
- NeuroFocus would never work as a real product name. Pebble. would.
- When someone asks "what app is that?" the answer "Pebble" is memorable. "NeuroFocus" is forgettable.

### The quirky personality it enables
- Chat placeholders: "Or just say hi." (the period is playful here)
- Nudges: "Starting is the hardest part. You're already here."
- Completion: "Done." (one word, one dot, deeply satisfying)
- The period becomes a design element — after every statement, the dot grounds it
- It's minimalist but has personality. Clean but not sterile.
